## Why Raging Surf Still Has Pricing Relevance in 2026

Sv3a released in Japan on September 22, 2023. That's nearly three years of price discovery. Sets at this age have usually found their floor: speculators rotated out, sealed product stopped moving at MSRP, and the remaining demand is genuine collectors targeting specific cards. PriceCharting's aggregate for the set sits around $343 (as of their last recorded index), and PSA has logged over 1,800 auction transactions totaling more than $69,000 in realized sales on sv3a cards alone. That's not a dead set; that's a set with stable collector demand anchored to a handful of high-value targets.

Booster boxes, for reference, are trading on TCGplayer as low as $69.99 (sealed listings, July 2026), down significantly from launch-window pricing. Pulling the key SARs from packs is now a losing EV proposition. Buy the singles.

The practical question for a buyer in 2026: does the PSA 10 premium justify submission costs? PSA's current standard service tier runs roughly $25-$30 per card (approximate, as of 2026 fee schedules). A raw NM copy at $300 going PSA 10 at $500 leaves a $170-$175 spread before fees and time. That's workable only if you're confident in your centering read. Japanese SARs from this era can print off-center, and a PSA 9 at $220-$250 erases the margin entirely.
