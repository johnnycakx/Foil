Garchomp ex #082/066 is the card that defines sv3a Raging Surf's secondary market in 2026; everything else in the set prices relative to it.

Raging Surf (set code sv3a) released September 22, 2023 as a 66-card Japanese mini-set focused on Tera-type mechanics and the Paldea coastal biome. The set punches well above its card count on chase density: four Special Illustration Rares (SIRs), two Illustration Rares with genuine collector demand, and a trainer SAR that outperforms most Pokémon SIRs in the same sleeve. If you're building a position in [japanese pokemon cards value](/japanese-pokemon-cards-value), sv3a is a compact, well-documented set worth knowing card by card.

This guide covers every card worth tracking by collector number, current price tier, and graded premium. PriceCharting and Pokellector list raw prices; TCG Collector pegs the set's aggregate market at roughly $346 in a full set build (as of their last index). This post goes deeper: grade spreads, which SIRs have meaningful PSA 10 premiums, and which cards the market is currently mispricing.

## The Four SIRs: Current Price Tiers

SIRs in sv3a run collector numbers beyond the set's printed 066 base. Japanese sets use the over-number system for secret rares. Here's where each one sits as of mid-2026:

| Card | Collector # | Raw (ungraded) | PSA 10 (approx.) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Garchomp ex SIR | 082/066 | $55-$70 | $160-$200 |
| Palafin ex SIR | 079/066 | $18-$26 | $55-$75 |
| Arboliva ex SIR | 080/066 | $12-$18 | $40-$60 |
| Clavell SAR (Trainer) | 081/066 | $30-$45 | $90-$130 |

Garchomp ex SIR carries a Nagashima Iyo illustration that rewards grading. Centering variance on the Japanese print run is relatively tight compared to sv3a's English equivalents, and a PSA 10 pop that was already lean going into 2026 has kept the ceiling firm. A clean PSA 10 has cleared $190 in recent eBay completed sales.

## The Illustration Rares Worth Tracking

Not every chase card in sv3a is an SIR. Two IRs trade with enough frequency to anchor a price.

**Garchomp ex IR (#067/066):** The standard full-art Garchomp. Raws move in the $8-$14 range. PSA 10s have sold in the $35-$50 window. It shares a Pokémon with the SIR, so collectors chasing a Garchomp binder page often pick up both, which props the IR's floor higher than its rarity would suggest standalone.

**Palafin ex IR (#068/066):** Less demand than Garchomp, but Palafin has a niche following. Raws sit around $5-$9. PSA 10 liquidity is thin; expect to wait on a sale.

The remaining holos and standard rares in sv3a trade under $5 raw and have negligible graded premiums. There's no mystery tier hiding in the middle of this set.

## What the Graded Ladder Actually Looks Like

PSA is the dominant grader for sv3a Japanese cards because the set released squarely in the 2023-2024 submission surge. BGS and CGC have smaller populations, which creates opportunity if you trust alternate-grader prices, but liquidity is genuinely thinner.

For Garchomp ex SIR specifically, the PSA 10-to-9 spread is meaningful. A PSA 9 raw-equivalent trades roughly 40-50% below a PSA 10. That gap is wide enough that submitting a card with visible print defects or a soft corner is an equity-destroying decision. Inspect name, set code (sv3a), and collector number (082/066) on every card before committing to a grading submission.

<Callout variant="warning">
**Don't identify sv3a cards by artwork alone.** The Garchomp ex SIR and Garchomp ex IR use different collector numbers (082/066 vs. 067/066) and price $40-$50 apart raw. Reading the three fields, name, set code, and collector number, is the only reliable method. Artwork-first identification is how misidentification happens on resale listings.
</Callout>

## The Booster Box: Sealed vs. Cracking Math

A sv3a Raging Surf booster box listed on TCGplayer in mid-2026 ranges from roughly $70 to $115 depending on seller and condition. K-TCG had sealed boxes at $138.99 as recently as their current listing (seller pricing fluctuates).

A box contains 30 packs at 5 cards each. Hit rate for an SIR is approximately 1-in-30 packs in the Japanese print (one SIR per box, no guarantee on which one). Expected value from cracking: if you assign equal weight to all four SIRs, the average SIR pull at current raw prices is roughly $28-$40. That's below a $75 box cost before you account for bulk commons.

The math favors buying singles directly unless you're opening for the experience. This is a set where sealed product has its own collector thesis (a 2023 mini-set with limited ongoing reprint risk), not an EV-positive cracking proposition.

## Quick Reference: How to Read Your sv3a Card

Every sv3a card carries its identification fields in consistent positions:

- **Name:** top of the card, in Japanese kanji/katakana (Foil's scanner reads this)
- **Set code:** printed as "sv3a" in small text at the card's bottom edge
- **Collector number:** printed as XXX/066 at the bottom right

If a listing doesn't specify all three fields, it's not a complete identification. A Garchomp ex with no collector number could be the $14 IR or the $65 SIR. That's a $51 ambiguity on a single card.
