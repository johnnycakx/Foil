The highest-value single from sv3a Raging Surf, the Garchomp ex Special Illustration Rare, has traded in a range of $180 to $260 raw in 2026, depending on centering and surface quality. That spread is wide enough that buying blind off a thumbnail is genuinely expensive.

Raging Surf (set code sv3a, released September 22, 2023) is a compact 62-card Japanese-exclusive set built around Water- and Fighting-type heavyweights. It sits within the broader <TopicLink href="/japanese-pokemon-cards-value">japanese pokemon cards value</TopicLink> ecosystem, where Japanese SIRs routinely trade at a discount to English alt arts before grading arb closes the gap. Two and a half years after release, the set has settled into a predictable price ladder, but the graded spread at PSA 9 vs. PSA 10 is where the real money is made or lost.

Every price below is sourced from eBay completed sales and TCGplayer market data as of mid-2026. None of these numbers come from a creator YouTube video.

## The sv3a Chase Tier: SIRs First

Special Illustration Rares are the ceiling of the set. There are four:

| Card | Collector # | Raw (NM) | PSA 9 | PSA 10 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Garchomp ex SIR | 071/062 | $180-$260 | $290-$340 | $650-$850 |
| Mew ex SIR | 072/062 | $90-$130 | $150-$190 | $380-$480 |
| Greninja ex SIR | 073/062 | $55-$80 | $90-$120 | $220-$300 |
| Palafin ex SIR | 074/062 | $30-$50 | $55-$80 | $130-$180 |

Garchomp ex (#071/062) is the set's anchor. PokeBeard noted in mid-2026 that Garchomp ex SIR was one of the more actively discussed Scarlet and Violet-era SIRs for potential grading arb, though he stopped short of calling a direction. The graded spread tells the real story: a PSA 10 at $700 on a raw card you paid $220 for is a compelling flip, if you can hit that grade. PSA's sv3a Raging Surf total auction value sits at $69,122 across 1,832 sales (per PSA's published data), which is modest for a set this age and implies most copies are sitting ungraded in collections.

<Callout variant="warning">The four SIR collector numbers are all above the base set count (062). If you are reading a card and the number shows 063-074, you are outside the base set. Artwork alone does not distinguish the Garchomp ex SIR from the standard Garchomp ex RR at a glance on a scan.</Callout>

Below the SIRs, Raging Surf has a tight Special Art Rare and gold card layer. These are smaller dollar amounts but higher volume: the cards most sellers actually move.

The Iono SAR (#069/062) punches above its weight. Iono is the most reprint-proof trainer in the set. She appears in Raging Surf as her first SAR treatment, and the $35-$55 raw range has been stable through Q1 and Q2 2026. PSA 10s at $140 represent a genuine grade premium; the raw-to-10 multiplier is roughly 2.5x to 3x, which is healthy but not enough to justify mass grading at current PSA fees unless you are confident in the card's condition.

<Callout variant="tip">The Iono SAR is one of the few cards in this set where centering variance is severe. A left-shifted Iono SAR will almost never hit PSA 10, and the front-to-back misalignment on some print runs is visible without a loupe. Check the collector number (069/062) and inspect all four borders before buying raw for grading.</Callout>

## Booster Box and Sealed Context

Sealed product for sv3a is not a 2026 growth story. Raging Surf booster boxes were listed as low as $69.99 on TCGplayer as of mid-2026, with standard listings around $114.99. For a set that is nearly three years old, that is a soft sealed market. The box has been opened aggressively, and singles supply is deep.

This matters for raw card buyers: deep box supply means raw card supply is not constrained. The $180 floor on a Garchomp ex SIR is a real floor only while PSA 10 demand stays above $600. If the graded market softens, raw prices follow within weeks.

PikaPikaPapa has pointed to sealed Scarlet and Violet-era Japanese boxes broadly as long-term holds, citing several boxes that have crossed $1,000 after years of sitting. Raging Surf is not in that conversation yet. The box MSRP was 180 yen per pack (10 packs per box, roughly $13-$15 USD at launch), and the current $70-$115 box range reflects a set that was neither short-printed nor underdistributed.

## Grading Economics for sv3a

The PSA population for sv3a Raging Surf is relatively thin given the set's age. That is a structural opportunity if you have high-grade raw copies: low pop plus strong demand for the Garchomp ex SIR at PSA 10 creates real upside. The math only works if you are submitting at economy tier fees and hitting 10s at a reasonable rate.

One honest note on the grade ladder: sv3a cards have minor silver-border printing quirks on some production runs. The silver foil border on the SIRs is prone to micro-scratches from pack pulling. A card that looks NM in-hand can grade PSA 8 because of foil hits invisible until the card is under direct light. Inspect name plus set code plus collector number to confirm identity, then immediately sleeve and inspect foil under a lamp before deciding whether to submit.
