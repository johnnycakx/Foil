## The frame (read first)
The bottleneck is audience, and you have capital — so paid acquisition is a legitimate, faster lever than organic alone. BUT the #1 newsletter mistake is scaling paid spend before the funnel is proven to convert + retain (you buy a number that doesn't engage, learn nothing, and pollute deliverability). So this is **not an audience-buying spree — it's a cheap experiment to find the one channel that converts a Pokémon collector into an engaged subscriber for the least money.** We just shipped the UTM attribution that makes this measurable to the dollar. Spend small, find the winner, *then* deploy the $9K into it.

Note the capital plan's own conclusion: for this niche, **creator co-created content likely out-converts cold display ads** (collectors buy on recommendation). So creators are in the test, not just Meta/Reddit.

## The 4 ad angles (test 3–4 creatives across the arms)
1. **Stop-scrubbing-eBay (the core job):** "eBay's full of $1.75 'NEAR MINT' Charizard junk. Foil finds the *one* real deal and emails you when your card hits your price. Free."
2. **Cognitive-offload (the compressed value prop):** "Tell us the Pokémon card you want. We watch eBay and email you the moment a real listing drops to your target price."
3. **The data hook (the unique signal):** "5 Pokémon cards are trading below their 30-day sold average right now. Get the weekly deal board, free."
4. **Founder credibility (the moat):** "A Level-4 TCGplayer seller built a tool that finds the best live Pokémon deals on eBay and alerts you when yours drops. Free."

Voice: calm, numbers-first, no hype, no em dashes (BRAND-VOICE Gate 12). Cowork drafts the copy + image directions; AI-generated images are fine for the test (no Higgsfield pipeline needed yet — that's a *scaling* tool for after a winner is proven).

## Success thresholds & the decision rule (from the capital plan's framework)
Measure two things per arm, not one:
- **CPA (cost per email signup)** — the headline number.
- **Engagement of those signups** — do ≥~25% open/click the first newsletter within a week? (Junk traffic signs up and ghosts; CPA alone lies.)

Decision rule:
- **CPA < $3 AND real engagement → scale that channel hard** (the plan's lesson: operators under-spend once unit economics work — don't be timid).
- **CPA $3–7 with engagement → workable; optimize creative, scale cautiously.**
- **CPA > $10 OR near-zero engagement → kill that arm.** Don't "fix it with more spend."
- The cheapest arm with real engagement wins → pour the $9K creator budget + paid scaling there.

Honest caveat: this validates **conversion**, not **profitability** — we don't know LTV yet (affiliate-revenue-per-sub unproven, no Pro tier). ROI proof comes once real affiliate revenue per subscriber shows up. Conversion is the right thing to learn first.
