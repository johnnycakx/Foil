## Strategic frame

- **Lead with the utility:** "the best price on any Pokemon card you want, instantly, free." The deals leaderboard + X bot are the hook and the traffic engine, not the whole product.
- **It is a distribution problem.** The product is mostly built; the bottleneck is traffic, indexing, and authority. GSC shows 7 of 220 pages indexed (203 "discovered, not crawled" = new-site authority problem, not a settings problem).
- **Affiliate = baseline/bootstrap. Subscription = the margin lever** (150 subs ~= 150K monthly visitors). Build subscription-READY now; flip it on once an audience exists.

## Subscription-ready design (build the seam NOW, flip later)

**Free tier (the funnel — never paywalled):** best price on any card (per-card pages), the deals leaderboard, browse/search, the weekly best-deals newsletter, and hourly/daily watchlist alerts. This is what ranks in Google, what the X bot drives traffic to, and what earns trust.

**Paid tier (~$10/mo, the wedge — flipped on later):**
- **Instant deal alerts:** watch unlimited cards, get notified the moment one drops below market (real-time vs the free batch). "Get the deal first."
- Full deal list (free shows the top N; paid shows all confident deals).
- Grading-arbitrage lane (PSA-gain plays) once gem-% data lands.
- CSV export of watchlist/deal history, advanced filters, more marketplaces as they come.
- Framing: "support the site + get deals first," not "pay for premium." Affiliate stays primary for free/organic traffic. Do NOT revive the founder-member tier.

**Design-now requirements (so the flip is a switch, not a rebuild):**
- Accounts-optional becomes accounts-ready: keep watchlists email-anchored, but structure the data so a user/account + a tier flag can layer on cleanly.
- Build the free/paid SEAM into the watchlist + alerts code now: an entitlement/tier check at the alert-frequency and watch-limit boundaries, defaulting everyone to free. Stripe test-mode plumbing already exists; live mode is ready post-Mercury.
- Instrument it: track engaged-free-user count and which features users hit limits on, so the demand signal that triggers the flip is measurable.

## The wedge spec — instant-alerts watchlist (for a future goal, gated)
- Free: watchlist alerts on the existing hourly/daily cron, capped watch count.
- Paid: near-real-time alert when a watched card's live ask crosses below its condition-matched sold reference (reuse the buy-signal), unlimited watches.
- Entitlement gate (tier flag) at: alert latency (batch vs instant), watch-count cap, deal-list depth, grading lane, export.
- Stripe: $10/mo recurring (live mode post-banking), a single allowed billing boundary, never auto-charge without explicit checkout.
- Gate to build/flip: an engaged-free-user threshold + observed demand (users hitting free limits or asking).
