# Foil — X founder-voice launch kit (prepared 2026-06-26)

Voice per `docs/BRAND-VOICE.md`: Matt Levine deadpan × active seller. Dry, never hypes, teaches the mechanics, short punch then the why. Real numbers only, every figure a real recent eBay sold average. No em dashes. No hype words (no "game-changer", "to the moon", "dive in"). Founder first person, "I run a Pokémon card store" (not the retired "Level-4" line).

> **Refresh the numbers right before posting.** Figures below are from the 2026-06-25 movers data. Regenerate (or read the live `market_movers`) the day you post so they match. PokeTrace is live until ~July 15.

> Value-first, not "I built a thing." A new account gets no traction from an announcement. Lead with something useful, let Foil be the reason it exists.

## 2. The launch thread

> Mechanics: X throttles reach on posts that lead with a link. Keep tweet 1 link-free; the link goes in the last tweet. Post as a native thread.

**1 (hook, no link):**
> I run a Pokémon card store. Every week I pull the cards whose Near Mint copies are now selling below their own 30-day average on eBay.
>
> Not predictions. Just what cooled off this week. A few that stood out.

**2:**
> Blastoise, Base Set, Near Mint. About $100 over the last 7 days against a $120 thirty-day average. Down 17% across 51 sales.
>
> 51 sales is the part that matters. That is a trend, not one lowball buyer.

**3:**
> Alakazam, Base Set, Near Mint. About $41 this week against a $48 average. Down 16% across 36 sales.
>
> The Base Set holo nobody watches while everyone argues about Charizard.

**4:**
> Ditto, Fossil, Near Mint. About $32 this week against a $36 average. Down 10% across 48 sales.
>
> Small move, steady volume. That is usually the kind that drifts back up, not the kind that craters.

**5 (the other side of the signal):**
> Not everything is down. Pikachu from 151 is up: about $96 this week against an $88 average, across 612 sales.
>
> High liquidity, real demand. When something moves on that much volume, the move is real.

**6 (CTA, link lives here):**
> I do this every week and send it as one email: the cards moving, where the deals are, and one valuation note worth knowing before you buy. Free, no spam.
>
> foiltcg.com/newsletter

## 4. After you post
- Reply to anyone who engages in the first hour. Reply velocity drives reach, and it is ToS-safe (manual, by you).
- Reply to your first newsletter subscribers personally.
- Follow + reply in the Pokémon-collector conversation daily. This is your job; the bot handles the daily posting.

## 5. How the daily cadence becomes hands-off (see chat)
The launch thread above is a one-time founder intro, posted by you. The recurring daily posts are the bot's job. Target workflow: bot drafts daily to Discord, you approve in one tap, it posts. Until the approval gate is built, options are (a) full-auto now (`X_BOT_LIVE=true`, no review) or (b) dry-run review for a week to tune the voice.

## 6. Honesty guardrails
- Every dollar figure from live data the day you post. Regenerate first.
- "Candidate worth a look," never "guaranteed flip."
- No em dashes, no hype words. The calm, honest register IS the brand.
