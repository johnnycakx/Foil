## Why a host should say yes (value props, in priority order)

1. **Completely hands-off.** Foil installs, stocks, restocks, services, and handles every customer
   issue. The host's staff never touch it.
2. **Zero cost, hands-off.** No purchase, no lease, no fees. Foil owns, stocks, and services the machine
   and handles the business licensing and sales-tax compliance for operating it. (Insurance/liability
   specifics are a call topic — NOT published on the site; see the OFF-SITE note in the FAQ.)
3. **Passive income.** The host earns a revenue share every month for space they're already paying for.
4. **New foot traffic + an experience.** A Pokémon machine pulls in collectors and a new customer
   type, creates buzz, and adds something competitors don't have.
5. **Tiny footprint, sleek.** Jukebox-sized, not a clunky snack machine. ~3–4 sq ft. Wall, pedestal, or
   freestanding.
6. **Risk-free trial.** Start with a no-commitment trial month. If it's not a fit for either side, Foil pulls
   the machine and relocates it. No contract required.

## How it works (host's view, 4 steps)

1. **You reach out / we talk.** Quick conversation about your space and foot traffic.
2. **We place the machine.** Foil sizes, delivers, mounts, stocks, and tests it. Install takes about an
   hour and doesn't disrupt your day.
3. **It runs itself.** Cashless touchscreen, real-time monitored, auto-refunds on any misfire, customer
   support handled by Foil via an on-machine QR code.
4. **You get paid.** Monthly revenue share via your preferred method, with sales analytics.

## Public FAQ (sanitized from the objection-rebuttal blueprint)

Use these as the FAQ section + AEO/structured-data Q&A. Answers are truthful and host-framed.

**What does the machine sell?**
Sealed Pokémon trading-card packs — a rotating mix of mid-tier and premium packs (roughly $18–$100).
We adjust the selection based on what's trending and what your customers respond to.

**How big is it / will it fit?**
About the size of a jukebox — roughly 3×2 ft, extending about a foot off the wall. We also offer a
freestanding tower. It needs only 3–4 sq ft of wall or floor space. We can show you exactly how it looks
in your space before anything is installed.

**What do I have to provide?**
Just the space (about 3–4 sq ft), a standard power outlet, and wifi. We handle everything else.

**What does it cost me?**
Nothing. There's no purchase, lease, or fee. The machine draws about as much power as a TV
(~$4/month).

**Does it have to be drilled into the wall?**
Not at all. Wall-mount, pedestal, or freestanding — whatever works best for your space. The freestanding
option needs no drilling.

**How do I get paid?**
A monthly revenue share via your preferred method — direct deposit, ACH, check, Zelle, Venmo, or Cash
App — with sales analytics so you can see exactly what's selling.

**What if a customer pays and nothing comes out?**
The machine has a guaranteed-drop system with a refund sensor. If product doesn't drop, the customer is
refunded automatically and the screen confirms it. Every machine also has a QR code so customers report
any issue directly to us — your staff never deals with refunds or complaints.

**Do I have to sign a contract?**
No. Most of our locations run on a handshake and a risk-free trial month. If you'd prefer something in
writing, we're happy to provide a simple placement agreement.

**What if it doesn't perform in my location?**
If it's not pulling the numbers we'd expect, we'll first try adjusting the product mix to your customers. If it
still isn't a fit, we pull it and relocate it — no cost or hassle to you.

**I don't really get kids in here.**
Pokémon isn't a kids' product anymore. Around 60% of buyers are adults 25–40 — collectors and resellers
with real money to spend. A $18 pack with a shot at pulling a card worth far more is an easy impulse buy
for that crowd.

**I already have a vending machine.**
Ours is a completely different category — sealed trading cards, not snacks or drinks — so it complements
what you have rather than competing with it.
