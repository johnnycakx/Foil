## Scaling with sales reps / scouts (performance-based)

- Pay only on results: **$350 flat per placed location + $0.50–$1.00 per pack** for the life of that machine.
  Offset the residual by raising pack price ~$1 (customer doesn't notice). Residual can be capped (1–2 yr)
  or perpetual — your call.
- **Hire for networks, not cold-calling ability.** The right rep already knows owners of target businesses
  (gas stations, smoke shops, restaurants, arcades). Vet: "Walk me through who you know that owns a
  fitting business." No network → don't hire.
- Find them: Indeed, ZipRecruiter, Facebook, Craigslist, and your personal network. Job-listing + sales-rep
  agreement templates are in `source-pdfs/`.
- **Always sign the Sales Representative Agreement before any intro.** Key terms in the template:
  independent contractor (1099), comp on Schedule A ($_ per pack), 1-yr auto-renewing term, 14-day
  termination, **12-month non-compete + non-solicit** in a defined restricted territory, confidentiality,
  indemnification. (The sample was signed by a "Megan Durrence" 07/29/2025 — that's the template's
  example, not a real Foil rep. Blank it before use.)

## Contracts / placement agreements

- **Default is a handshake + trial month.** Use a written agreement when (a) they ask, (b) corporate/chain,
  or (c) the owner seems sketchy. Call it a **"placement agreement,"** not a "contract" (less friction).
- **Two templates on file:**
  - **Simple Contract** — friendly, short: 24-mo term auto-renewing 12-mo, revenue-share %, operator
    supplies/services/insures, host provides space+power, mutual 30-day termination, exclusivity on trading-
    card sales.
  - **Hardcore Contract** — aggressive operator-protective: 5-yr term auto-renewing 5-yr (90-day non-
    renewal notice), **liquidated damages = 50% of remaining-term revenue**, exclusivity, **customer bears
    repair/replacement for damage/theft**, indemnification, jury-trial waiver, broad operator termination
    rights. Heavy — likely overkill/off-putting for small handshake locations; reserve for high-value or
    corporate deals, and have a lawyer review before use.
- Reality from the blueprint: contracts rarely get enforced for vending; relationship + service is what keeps
  the machine on the wall. **Disclaimer: not legal advice; CA-specific terms should be lawyer-reviewed.**

## Entity formation — LLC + EIN (HIGH PRIORITY, ties to doc 05)

- The blueprint includes a **research prompt** (in `source-pdfs/Getting LLC and EIN Prompt`) to run in
  ChatGPT/Perplexity with web search, restricted to **.gov sources only**, to get a step-by-step LLC + EIN
  guide for a given state. Good template; for **California** it would cover: CA Secretary of State LLC filing
  (Articles of Organization, Form LLC-1), the **$800 annual CA franchise tax**, Statement of Information,
  registered agent, the **free IRS EIN** application, and CA Dept. of Tax & Fee Administration (CDTFA)
  seller's permit / resale certificate.
- **COO recommendation:** forming the **CA LLC + EIN + CDTFA seller's permit** is a near-term priority —
  it unblocks business banking, NAYAX KYC, clean books, the resale (tax-free inventory) certificate, and the
  Section 179 / QBI tax posture in doc 05. Run the prompt for California, then confirm with the accountant
  (Chad Kufro, doc 05). The LLC name also becomes the entity on the website's legal/privacy pages.

## Mindset (founder operating frame)

- **You're not a salesman — you're offering a free, money-making amenity** and educating owners about
  Pokémon demand. Believe in the product; you're doing them a favor.
- "If you have a location, the money follows" — machines can be financed (half-down, equipment financing,
  BNPL, supplier in-house) once a spot is locked. **Land the location first.**
- It's simple but not easy: outreach volume + consistency + CRM notes are the work. Nobody does it for you.
