## The core framing (John's insight, correct)

The blueprint's Sourcing Product Guide (eBay lots, TCGplayer, Facebook Marketplace, local shops, retail
scalping) is **buying at retail/secondary market and upcharging for the machine's convenience** — not
wholesale distribution at cost. It works (the model profits at ~$10+/pack at market), but real **wholesale
distribution** is the margin unlock. This doc plans the path to wholesale while keeping arbitrage as the
bridge.

## The gating document: CA Seller's Permit (resale certificate)

Every legit distributor requires a **valid resale certificate + state sales-tax docs** before opening an
account. It also lets Foil buy inventory **tax-free for resale**. John has the **LLC (Foil TCG, LLC) + EIN**;
the missing piece is the **California Seller's Permit** from CDTFA.

- **Register:** https://cdtfa.ca.gov/services/registration.htm → "Register Online" → "Register a New
  Business Activity." **Free**, and **many applicants get the permit immediately**.
- **Have ready:** LLC info + EIN, John's driver-license # + SSN (responsible party), business address
  (2710 Southern Hills Ct, Fairfield), bank account (Mercury), and an estimated monthly/annual sales figure.
- Phone help: CDTFA 1-800-400-7115.
- **This is now the top operational unlock** — it gates wholesale accounts AND tax-free buying. Do it first.

## ⚠️ GTS Distribution — likely NOT a fit as a vending operator (do not misrepresent)

GTS's pre-screening form (reviewed 2026-06-13) only accepts two business types: **"Brick & Mortar Store
Front"** or **"Breaker."** It explicitly states: *not accepting new Online-only Retailer Accounts*, and *no
3rd-party internet retailers (Amazon/eBay) since 2018.*

- **A vending-machine operator is neither** a storefront nor a breaker. There is no truthful category.
- **Do NOT select "Brick & Mortar Store Front"** — claiming a storefront Foil doesn't have is a
  misrepresentation that gets the account denied on verification (they require resale docs tied to a
  location) or terminated later. Not worth it.
- **Honest move:** email **customercare@gtsdistribution.com** first — "I operate trading-card vending
  machines placed in retail businesses (physical retail, not online-only, no single storefront). Do vending
  operators qualify, and under which category?" Let them confirm a path before submitting. Mention a
  Gage-network referral in "How did you hear about GTS" if one exists.
- Realistic read: GTS is large + selective + built for storefronts/breakers. It may simply say no. That's
  fine — the paperwork below applies to every distributor, and smaller regional ones are often more flexible.

## The hard truth: Pokémon is allocated

Sealed Pokémon demand massively exceeds supply, so distributors **allocate** it — prioritizing established
retailers with order history. A brand-new small account often gets approved but receives **little or no
Pokémon allocation** (plenty of Magic/Lorcana/supplies, but Pokémon gated). So a wholesale account is
**necessary but not sufficient** for cheap Pokémon at volume. This is *why* the blueprint defaults to
market arbitrage. Set expectations accordingly.

## Realistic path (in order)

1. **Get the CA Seller's Permit now** (link above). Gates everything.
2. **Ask Gage / Coach AI about the program's supplier + a group-buy / program wholesale rate.** Likely the
   **best near-term lever** — the network's combined volume commands allocation + pricing a solo new
   account can't. (Blueprint "Method 1: our supplier link" hints at a program arrangement.)
3. **Apply to distributors** once the permit's in hand. Use Pokémon's official authorized-distributor list
   (support.pokemon.com → distributors). Try GTS only after the eligibility email; apply to 1–2 **regional
   TCG distributors** (often onboard new small accounts more easily). Expect thin Pokémon allocation at
   first; build order history to grow it.
4. **Bridge with market arbitrage** (eBay lots / FB Marketplace / retail drops per the blueprint) to fund
   and stock the first machines. The model profits at market price; wholesale is the optimization, not a
   day-one requirement.
5. **Compound:** more machines + distributor order history → better allocation → lower COGS → fatter margin.
